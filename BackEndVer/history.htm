Who We Are


The Lower Mainland Christmas Bureau (LMCB) was originally established in 1930 as the Christmas Exchange. Today it serves as a centralized, year round toy warehouse for toys and gifts to be gathered, sorted, stored and shipped to Christmas Bureaus and Community agencies, across the entire metro Vancouver area. We ship up to 100,000 items every year.


We also serve as the local Christmas Bureau for Vancouver. The LMCB distributes toys, clothes and food vouchers to over a 1000 low income families in Vancouver during the holiday season.


Our Vision


Christmas is about family and friends and the experience of sharing and giving. It should be a happy time for everyone, especially for children. For over eighty-five years our number one goal has been to ensure that every child in our community has a smile on their face on Christmas Day.


Our Mission


Here at the Lower Mainland Christmas Bureau, our mission is to empower families in need to create Christmas experiences for their kids through the generosity of community.


As a registered non-profit organization, the Lower Mainland Christmas Bureau relies on volunteers and a number of fundraising events throughout the year to support our goals.


Our History


1930 Established as the Christmas Exchange

1939 Renamed the Christmas Cheer Committee as part of the Vancouver Council of Social Agencies

1946 Community Chest and Council formed Christmas Cheer Committee part of central services

1947 Camping Referral Project established

1953 First used title “Christmas Bureau”

1970 Camping Referral Project and Christmas Bureau combined to one committee

1980 United Way approves terms of reference for Christmas and Camping Bureaus

1981 Christmas and Camping programs are relocated to Commercial Drive location

1984 United Way sets up committee to consider future of Bureau & commissions the historical review of the Christmas and Camping Bureaus by K Wyndham Brown

1989 Lower Mainland Christmas Bureau and Lower Mainland Camping Bureaus are registered as separate British Columbia Societies

1993 LMCB relocates to Napier Street Facility

2003 Lower Mainland Camping Bureau Society becomes part of Christmas Bureau

2008 LMCB moves to Pandora Street location
